A Billion Lonely People

Article exploring the latest scientific research on the global rise in reported loneliness, drawing on media reporting, academic papers, and background interviews with Alma Economics social researchers.

This thought-leadership article was researched and authored as part of Alma Economics’ communications strategy, exploring one of the most pressing social issues of our time: the so-called “global loneliness epidemic.” The piece drew on a wide range of sources including the latest academic research, WHO reports, and media coverage to provide a nuanced, data-driven exploration of the topic.

The article examined the scale of the problem — with estimates suggesting over a billion people worldwide experience significant loneliness — and explored the complex web of factors driving it, from urbanisation and technological change to shifting social structures and demographic trends.

Background interviews with Alma Economics social researchers provided additional depth and expert perspective on the economic and public health dimensions of loneliness. The piece was instrumental in promoting thought-leadership articles as a key element of the company’s communications strategy, demonstrating the value of long-form content in building the firm’s reputation and reach.
